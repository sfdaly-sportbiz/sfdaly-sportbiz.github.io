I direct the Sport Business undergraduate and graduate programs at the University of Oklahoma's Price College of Business, where I teach analytics, leadership, global sport, and venue and event operations.1Programs include the Sports Business major/minor, the Esports Certificate, and the Sports Business Graduate Certificate. Before Oklahoma, I spent over a decade at Johnson & Wales University building sport and event management programs from the ground up, including a run as Interim Assistant Dean. Outside the classroom, I've built NIL valuation models for college athletics, run economic impact studies for events like the PGA Korn Ferry Tour, and coached leadership development for organizations that have nothing to do with sport at all.

A Longer Note

My path here started on a volleyball court, not in a classroom — I coached college volleyball for seven years before moving into sport administration full time. That coaching background shows up in almost everything I've studied since: my doctoral dissertation looked at servant leadership and employee outcomes in NCAA Division III athletics, and a good chunk of my writing and consulting since has circled the same question — what actually makes people willing to follow someone, and how do you build an organization where that's possible.

Right now that shows up in two places. At Oklahoma, I'm building out a Center for Sports Business Excellence and working with OU Athletics on NIL valuation and training for incoming student-athletes. Outside the university, I became a certified Cultural Intelligence Coach Trainer in 2024, and most of my current consulting and writing — including a piece in the Harvard Business Review — is about what it actually takes for a leader or an organization to work well across cultural lines, not just claim to.
