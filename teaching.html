Approach

Teaching Philosophy

Most of what I teach, I learned by doing it first — seven years coaching college volleyball before I ever wrote a syllabus, and every course since has been built to get students into real organizations as fast as possible, not just reading about them.2Programs I've built to do exactly this include the ESPN X Games student immersion program and USOC Olympic team-processing placements — both giving students paid, on-site operations experience. My doctoral minor in applied statistics and research methods shapes the analytics side; my certification as a Cultural Intelligence Coach Trainer shapes how I teach leadership across cultural lines. Outcomes assessment isn't an afterthought for me — I've led it at the department, college, and university level at two institutions, and I build every course backward from what a student should be able to do with an employer on day one.
