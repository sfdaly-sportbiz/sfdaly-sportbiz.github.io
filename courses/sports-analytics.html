Course · MGT 3223

Sports Analytics

Undergraduate Quantitative
Course Description

Students will use analytics to study a wide variety of issues affecting the sport industry. Topics examined include player performance measurement; in-game decision making; player selection/team building; and general administration such as marketing, pricing, contracts, and stadium management. Students will learn not only how the application of analytics has improved each of these areas within the professional and collegiate sport industry, but also how analytics can improve decision making in any other field of business.

Course Objectives
  • Describe the role of data analysis for modern day sport organizations.
  • Identify the appropriate analysis and measurement tool that should be used to answer a sport industry question.
  • Explain what value data provides for strategic decision-making in the sport industry (e.g. keeping a player, signing a new player, entering a new broadcast agreement).
  • Communicate orally and visually data analysis results and interpretations.
  • Demonstrate the ability to gather, analyze, interpret, and communicate results related to sports business analytics.
